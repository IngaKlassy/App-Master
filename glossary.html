Glossary

Board

is where you'll save related pins. For example, you could create a board for the different recipes you find online. (Pinterest)

Celebrity Syndrome

refers to a situation in which a non-celebrity mistakenly believes he or she is a celebrity. Users with celebrity syndrome will not follow a significant number of Twitter users. (Twitter)

Daily Twitamin

means a tweet that contains useful knowledge that is easy to take in and remember. It is a "daily vitamin" for Twitterers. (Twitter)

Drunktwittering

is the act of posting on Twitter while intoxicated. (Twitter)

Flashtagram

is a flash mob that occurs on Instagram, when a group of Intagramers post predetermined images at a set time to amuse and confuse followers, or show support for a particular cause or event. (Instagram)

Filters

are additions that users can select to decorate their Snaps. To add a filter, simply snap a photo or a video then swipe left to see what filters are available. (Snapchat)

Friend

is someone you are connected to on Facebook. (Facebook)

Friending

is the act of sending someone a friend request; all friendships must be confirmed by both people in order for the friendship to be official on Facebook. (Facebook)

Hashtag

is a way of organizing your updates for Twitter search engines. Users prefix a message with a community-driven hashtag to enable others to discover relevant posts. One commonly used hashtag is #followfriday where users network by providing the names of their favourite people to follow on Twitter. (Twitter)

Hashtag Abuser

when you overuse the hashtag symbol. Includes having more hashtags than words in your post, adding the prefix "insta" to everything, tagging things that aren't really related, and making hashtags longer than sentences. (Instagram)

Instagram Moment

is a moment where the setting, or what's happening is just so surreal, that it would be great for Instagram. (Instagram)

Insta-lerking

is going more than 25 weeks deep into someone that you fantasize about's Instagram Page and not liking any pictures. (Instagram)

Instapire

when someone has made a small empire on Instagram by gaining a ridiculous amount of followers by posting hundreds of selfies. (Instagram)

Live

is a great way to see what's happening around the world from a stranger's point of view. Live is basically a Snapchat Story of a place with a Snapchatter as a tour guide. (Snapchat)

News Feed

is continuous stream of updates about your friends' activities on and off Facebook. It appears on your Home Page. (Facebook)

Pin

is visual bookmark that links from Pinterest to another website. (Pinterest)

Pinner

users of Pinterest are commonly called pinners. Whenever ypu find pinners you like, you can choose to follow their pins. This way, whenever they share new pins, you'll see them on your homepage. (Pinterest)

Poke

is a casual gesture that means "I'm thinking of you". The person you poke receives the poke on her Home page when she logs in and has the choice to poke yo back. Only she (and not her friends) is aware that you poked her. (Facebook)

Re-pin

means to save. When you save a pin you discover on Pinterest to one of your own boards. (Pinterest)

Retweet

abbreviated as RT, is used on Twitter to show you are tweeting something that was posted by another user. The format is RT @username where username is the Twitter name of the person you are retwitting. (Twitter)

Snap

is the basic unit of content on Snapchat, a Snap is a video or a picture captured and shared on the Snapchat app. (Snapchat)

Snapcash

is the result of partnershp between Snapchat and Square, Snapchat allows users to transfer money to their friends. To access Snapcash, users must enter their debit card information, then text "$ + the amount they want to send" (e.g. "$5" for 5 dollars) to another Snapchat user. (Snapchat)

Source Link

is the URL of the website where a pin comes from. (Pinterest)

Status Update

is a short post you share on Facebook talking about what you are doing, thinking, or feeling at the moment. People update their status about everything from what they're eating to sharing memories about a loved one to advocating for a particular political position. (Facebook)

Story

Snaps shared to all of a user's Snapchat friends are compiled into a series of photos or videos called a Story. Unlike individual Snaps, which disappear almost instantly, Stories stay on the app for 24 hours. Snapchat users may also download their own Stories to keep a permanent record of each day's event, if desired. (Snapchat)

Timeline

is your Profile Page. It contains all your posts, your photos and videos, a list of your friends, your favourite activities and interests, and any biographical info you've chosen o share. This is where your friends can leave you messages and where you can update the world with anything you feel like sharing. (Facebook)
